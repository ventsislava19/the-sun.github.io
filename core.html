Core

Due to the massive mass of the sun, its' internal structure is extremely compressed. The core, for example, is 150 times more compressed than water. Also, the core is the hottest layer in the Sun - reaching 13,000,000 K. Because of this, heat collected in it becomes the ideal environment for nuclear reactions to occur. These reactions consist of the joining of 4 hydrogen atoms into 1 helium atom. In this reaction the mass of the 4 hydrogen atoms is greater than the mass of the helium atom. It is this difference in the masses that becomes the energy of the sun.

Fun fact

The Sun was formed about 4.6 billion years ago from the collapse of a part of a giant molecular cloud that consisted mostly of hydrogen and helium.
