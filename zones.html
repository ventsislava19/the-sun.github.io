Radiative zone & Convective zone

Energy from the core must pass through all other layers to exit through the photosphere and spread out into space. The energy first passes through the Radiative Zone where it travels in the form of photons. These photons move chaotically, because of their collision with the atoms in the matter of the Convective zone. They move through the Radiative Zone, and after reaching the Convection Zone, the energy is taken up by Convection Currents, with the help of which, it reaches the photosphere, from where it comes out in the form of light.

This process takes 100,000-200,000 years. When the energy leaves the photosphere as light, it takes 8 minutes for it to reach the Earth and then it continues to travel through the galaxy and the universe as solar wind.

Fun fact

Convection Currents are called "currents" , because of the fact that the Sun is made out of matter in the state of plasma-gas.
