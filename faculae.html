Faculae

Around the edges of sunspots, the magnetic fields of the fluxes are highly concentrated. This can energize the plasma even more, causing it to heat up. This creates a bright rim around sunspots called Faculae (latin for "small torch"). The dark parts of sunspots obscure light from the Sun, but faculae can be very intense and therefore compensate for these dark spots and even add light.
Therefore, sunspots increase the energy output of the Sun.
