Characteristics

The Sun is a star of spectral class G2V, often informally described as a yellow dwarf, and it contains about 99.8% of the total mass of the Solar System. It has an almost perfectly spherical shape and is composed of plasma. Chemically, about 3/4 of the Sun's mass is hydrogen, with the remainder being mostly helium and the other heavier elements (1.69%). Its' diameter is about 1,392,000 km.
The period of actual rotation of the Sun is approximately 25.6 days at the equator and 33.5 days at the poles. At the same time, due to the constantly changing position of the Earth in its' rotation around the Sun, the apparent rotation of the star from our planet is about 28 days.
The solar wind created by the Sun's corona reaches about 100 AU and the light from the Sun travels to Earth in about 8 minutes.

Fun fact

The Solar System is surrounded by a Local Interstellar Cloud, although it is not clear if it is embedded in the Local Interstellar Cloud or if it lies just outside the cloud's edge.
