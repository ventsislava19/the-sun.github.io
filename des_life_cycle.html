01Let's learn about the sun's main characteristics and predict its' future!

Life span

The Sun is a star far smaller than the blue giants. It was formed 4.6 billion years ago, and for a typical G2 star its' existence expectancy is about 10 billion years.

The Sun is not massive enough to explode as a supernova would. Instead, after another 4 to 5 billion years, it will become a red giant, depleting the hydrogen in its' core. Then it will begin to convert helium into carbon and the temperature of its' core will rise.

The high temperature will cause the Sun's outer convection layers to "swell", possibly reaching as far as Earth's orbit. However, recent research suggests that as a result of the Sun's intense mass loss during inflation, our planet will move into a higher orbit. Depleting the helium in its' core, the Sun will continue to contract and expand, losing some of its' outer layers with each completed cycle, until it becomes a white dwarf composed of hydrogen.

Fun fact

The sun still baffles scientists. In the field of astronomy there's many unsolved problems, for example: ''Why is the Sun's corona so much hotter than the Sun's surface?'' or ''How could the early Earth have had liquid water if the Sun's output is predicted to have only been 70% as intense as it is today?''.
