Photosphere

After the chromosphere comes the photosphere. It is the "surface" of the sun - although when we think of a surface, we think of a hard shell, here the photosphere is nothing like that. It is more of a boundary from which the light of the Sun begins to emerge.

The Sun is made of plasma of varying density. This density decreases from the core to the upper layers. Therefore, the photosphere is not a rigid boundary, but simply a layer with suitable temperature and low density for emitting light. This suitable temperature is around 6000K.

Fun fact

It is in this layer of the Sun, in which, the helium is found through observations of the optical spectrum of the Sun.
