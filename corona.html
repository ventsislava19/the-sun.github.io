Corona

The outermost layer of the sun is the corona. In the picture it is the white light coming from the sun. Although it is only around the sun, it is still considered part of its' structure. It can only be observed during a solar eclipse with special gear. In principle, it is dangerous for a person to observe a solar eclipse without precautions, because the pupils dilate, causing more light to be absorbed by them.

During the eclipse, the Moon obscures the main part of the Sun and the only visible structure is the coronal glow. Although we feel no pain when looking directly at the sun in this phase, there is still dangerous radiation that can cause partial or total blindness.

The temperature of the corona reaches 100,000K.

Fun fact

Interestingly, the corona has a higher temperature than the layer below it (the photosphere). This fascinating difference still remains a mystery to researchers.
